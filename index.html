    What is a Greenhouse?

    Definition of Greenhouses

    A greenhouse is a structure designed to grow plants in a controlled environment. It is usually made of transparent materials like glass or plastic that allow sunlight to enter while trapping heat inside. This warmth helps maintain a stable temperature, creating ideal conditions for plant growth even when the weather outside is unfavorable.

    Greenhouses are used to protect plants from extreme temperatures, wind, and pests, extend growing seasons, and improve plant health and productivity.
